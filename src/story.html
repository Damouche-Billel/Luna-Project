The Story of LUNA

Luna Hope, a designer and painter obsessed with the night, paints her obsession at the edge of town, until she discovers her paintings appeared in mysterious poems.

A Romance Written in Paint & Poetry

Two artists, one destiny under the London moon

Storyline
A Story Unfolds

LUNA

Luna, a designer and hobby painter, is obsessed with the night. She often paints late above the city, yearning for a soul mate —a muse who inspires her and whom she inspires in return.

She notices white paper with black-ink printed verses on the wall. To her surprise, the poems are about her paintings. She dares not paint anymore, fearing that someone is following her, and she begins selling her artwork.

Meanwhile, the mysterious poet, Tyler Waves, illegally posts his poems on the street walls. One evening, he notices a painting behind a window that precisely depicts the poem he is about to post that night. He invites Luna to meet him in his poem in the light of the Moon.

That night, her friend Tara convinced her to go to the edge of the town to meet this mysterious poet.

Under the soft glow of London's streetlights and the watchful eye of the moon, two artistic souls prepare to discover that sometimes the most beautiful stories are written not in paint or poetry alone, but in the magical space where both converge.

A Message
From the
Director

People these days don't appreciate details. They scroll through their screens, posts and videos. Left, right, left or right? You know what I'm talking about, right? They only see the surface, the fast-paced videos and the perfect lifestyle that most people pretend to have.

What if there were an app where we could decide who we liked or disliked, not based on their face and appearance? But ultimately, our inner selves would decide who we matched with, whether it was a photo, a song, a painting or a poem that you can attach to. Maybe we would actually find our soulmate.

This film beautifully embodies this idea by stripping away the technical details as two people connected by their art finally find each other.

— Director, LUNA
